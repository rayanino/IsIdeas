# Codex Start Here

Use this file when beginning any background repo work with Codex.

## Mission
You are working inside a high-rigor idea workshop for long-horizon Islamic-study software design.

Your job is not to invent the strategic standard.
Your job is to execute bounded tasks against the workshop's existing standard.

## Read These First
Before changing anything, inspect:
- `README.md`
- `CLAUDE.md`
- `principles/QUALITY_BAR.md`
- `principles/ANTI_CHAOS_RULES.md`
- `workflows/CONSISTENCY_AND_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH.md`
- `workflows/WORKSPACE_CONTRACT.md`
- `workflows/CLAUDE_HANDOFF_STANDARD.md`
- the specific task packet you were given

## Default Behavior
- prefer bounded edits over broad rewrites
- preserve IDs, slugs, and portfolio state unless explicitly instructed otherwise
- do not promote idea stages on your own
- do not invent new serious ideas on your own
- do not flatten dossier nuance into shallow summaries
- prefer opening a PR over silently changing durable state without review

## What Good Execution Looks Like
A good Codex task result is:
- precise
- reviewable
- consistent with workshop rules
- narrow enough to evaluate
- and helpful without pretending to make strategic judgments

## When To Be Careful
Slow down and be extra conservative when a task touches:
- idea stage labels
- priority bands
- active focus
- parked / rejected status
- `kr` boundaries
- or idea dossiers in ways that affect strategic interpretation

## Golden Rule
If the task would require deciding whether an idea is extraordinary, strategically worthy, or safe enough from a scholarly-risk perspective, stop and defer that judgment for ChatGPT or the human reviewer.
