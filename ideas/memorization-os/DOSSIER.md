# Memorization OS — Dossier

## Working Thesis
Build a memorization management system tailored to Islamic texts that helps preserve memorized material over years through better segmentation, review timing, and weak-point visibility.

## Why This Might Matter
Memorization remains central in many serious study paths, and poor review discipline quietly destroys previous gains.

## The Bottleneck
Students often memorize with inconsistent review structures, weak visibility into fragile sections, and no durable system for preserving what was already hard to acquire.

## Why This Might Be Strong
- attacks a high-value recurring bottleneck
- supports one of the few domains where internalization matters deeply
- can create long-term compounding benefit if honest and sustainable
- complements `kr` rather than duplicating it

## Why This Might Be Weak
- retention measurement may be noisy and easy to overstate
- bad segmentation could make the system clumsy or misleading
- too much analytics could distract from actual memorization

## Main Risks
The system could optimize for app interaction rather than true memorization quality, or it could project fake precision about recall strength.

## Relationship To `kr`
`kr` can provide the structured texts, sources, and excerpt-adjacent substrate. This system should manage memorization behavior and review over time, not substrate preservation itself.

## Current Open Questions
- what is the right memorization unit for different text types?
- what review strategy is honest and sustainable?
- what should be teacher-adjustable?
- which analytics are genuinely useful rather than vanity?

## Current Judgment
Strong but immature.

## Next Move
Deepen the dossier until segmentation, review logic, and anti-fake-precision constraints are much sharper.
