# Question & Confusion Ledger — Dossier

## Working Thesis
Build a system that captures, classifies, and preserves points of confusion so they can be resolved deliberately instead of being forgotten or blurred.

## Why This Might Matter
A large part of serious learning is noticing exactly what is not yet understood and dealing with it carefully.

## The Bottleneck
Students accumulate unresolved questions and weak points of understanding faster than they can resolve them. Without a disciplined system, these gaps are forgotten, repeated, or mistaken for understanding.

## Why This Might Be Strong
- attacks a central learning bottleneck rather than a cosmetic one
- improves disciplined gap closure
- could improve the quality of future teacher questions
- compounds with topic, source, and excerpt structure from `kr`

## Why This Might Be Weak
- classification may become overcomplicated
- stored notes may be mistaken for real resolution
- the system could become a dumping ground rather than a clarity tool

## Main Risks
The system could create false closure if it treats retrieval, note-taking, or superficial paraphrase as genuine resolution.

## Relationship To `kr`
`kr` can provide source, excerpt, and topic linkage. This system should manage confusion capture, resolution status, and question workflow rather than re-implement the knowledge substrate.

## Current Open Questions
- what confusion taxonomy is actually useful in practice?
- what counts as tentative versus real resolution?
- how should unresolved items be prioritized?
- how much structure can be imposed without slowing capture?

## Current Judgment
Strong but immature.

## Next Move
Deepen the dossier until the resolution model, classification scheme, and false-closure safeguards are much sharper.
