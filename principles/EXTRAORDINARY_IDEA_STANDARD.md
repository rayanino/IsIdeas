# Extraordinary Idea Standard

This repository is not aiming for merely decent ideas.
It is trying to discover ideas that materially change the ceiling of serious Islamic study.

That means the standard must be unusually high.

## What Counts As Extraordinary

An extraordinary idea usually does several of the following at once:
- attacks a deep recurring bottleneck rather than a surface annoyance
- compounds over years rather than offering a one-time convenience
- changes the quality of study, not just the speed of file access
- creates leverage that would have been unusually hard or impossible without software
- strengthens disciplined learning, correctness, retention, comparison, or gap closure
- fits coherently into the broader study operating system

## What Does Not Count

Do not confuse extraordinary with:
- flashy UI
- large scope
- novelty alone
- automation for its own sake
- generic study-productivity features with Islamic terminology pasted on top
- technically impressive but strategically weak workflows

## The Main Test

A brutal question to ask of any candidate idea:

> If this worked beautifully, would it materially improve the depth, order, retention, or correctness of study over many years?

If the answer is weak, the idea is not extraordinary.

## Secondary Test

> Would a serious student meaningfully suffer if this system did not exist?

Not every strong idea must pass this at the highest level, but extraordinary ideas tend to create an absence you can clearly feel once imagined.

## Strategic Preference

Prefer ideas that:
- reduce long-term cognitive waste
- preserve scarce scholarly effort
- prevent repeated failure modes
- make structured knowledge actually more usable
- or strengthen the student rather than merely entertaining the student

## Warning

An idea can be intelligent, difficult to build, and still not deserve the three-year opportunity cost.
The bar here is not whether an idea is good.
The bar is whether it is worthy.
