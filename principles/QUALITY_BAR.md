# Quality Bar

This repository exists to protect a rare opportunity: multiple years of preparation before serious Islamic study begins.

Because of that, the standard must be severe.

## The Core Principle

A weak idea that consumes design time is not harmless.
It competes against ideas that may genuinely matter.

So the repository should be biased toward:
- pressure-testing,
- pruning,
- delaying promotion,
- and demanding clarity.

## What Makes An Idea Strong

A strong idea usually has most of the following:
- attacks a real recurring study bottleneck,
- creates leverage over years, not days,
- is genuinely Islamic-study-specific or deeply adapted,
- improves correctness or disciplined learning,
- compounds with `kr` or future systems,
- can be modeled clearly,
- has an MVP that proves something real,
- and has bounded scholarly-risk.

## What Makes An Idea Weak

Be suspicious of ideas that are:
- merely cool,
- generic productivity wrapped in Islamic language,
- hard to evaluate,
- impossible to scope,
- heavily dependent on magical inference,
- mostly UI fantasy,
- or useful only in rare edge cases.

## Promotion Gates

### Spark → Incubating
Only if the idea seems plausibly high-leverage.

### Incubating → Dossier
Only if the idea is worth serious discussion, not just a passing thought.

### Dossier → Spec-ready
Only if the problem, leverage, boundaries, data model, and risks are becoming concrete.

### Spec-ready → Handoff-ready
Only if the spec is implementation-grade and the MVP can be built without hidden ambiguity.

## Required Intellectual Honesty

You must be willing to say:
- this idea is weaker than it first appeared,
- this should be parked,
- this is too early,
- this depends on missing substrate,
- or this is strategically inferior to another path.

## Desired Bias

Prefer:
- fewer ideas,
- deeper dossiers,
- clearer specs,
- stronger interconnection,
- and ruthless rejection of noise.

## Final Test

A good question to ask of any idea:

> If this worked beautifully, would it materially change the quality of study over years?

If the answer is weak, the idea probably is too.
