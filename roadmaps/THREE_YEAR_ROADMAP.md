# Three-Year Roadmap

This roadmap assumes the user is preparing the software environment during a computer science degree, with serious Islamic study becoming the primary focus after graduation.

The goal is not to ship the most software.
The goal is to enter the study phase with an unusually powerful, disciplined, and trustworthy software environment.

## Year 1 — Foundations and Shared Primitives

### Objective
Establish the architecture, vocabulary, and first high-leverage specifications.

### Priorities
- stabilize `kr` directionally as the substrate,
- define shared primitives across the portfolio,
- write high-quality specs for first-wave systems,
- reject weak ideas aggressively.

### Deliverables
- method docs completed,
- glossary and shared terminology,
- first five serious specs,
- shared data-contract draft across portfolio.

### Success Condition
At the end of Year 1, the project should have a coherent blueprint rather than just enthusiasm.

## Year 2 — First Executable Leverage Systems

### Objective
Convert the best specs into working MVPs.

### Priorities
- Curriculum Architect MVP,
- Memorization OS MVP,
- Question & Confusion Ledger MVP,
- begin shared services and common IDs,
- validate real workflows, not toy demos.

### Deliverables
- 2 to 3 working systems with shared primitives,
- real test scenarios,
- revised specs based on actual friction,
- first integration surfaces with `kr`.

### Success Condition
At the end of Year 2, the environment should help with actual study behavior, not just represent plans on paper.

## Year 3 — Integration and Scholarly Leverage

### Objective
Tighten the stack into a coherent study operating system and add higher-order advantage tools.

### Priorities
- evidence graph,
- khilaf mapper,
- stronger review and comparison flows,
- dashboards only where they genuinely help,
- prepare the environment for real post-graduation use.

### Deliverables
- integrated portfolio stack,
- evidence-aware cross-app flows,
- second-wave leverage tools,
- implementation backlog for the first post-graduation phase.

### Success Condition
At the end of Year 3, the user should enter serious study with a mature environment that meaningfully reduces chaos, forgetfulness, and fragmentation.

## Standing Rules Across All Three Years

- protect `kr` quality,
- do not reward idea quantity over design quality,
- do not build what cannot be evaluated,
- keep scholarly-risk boundaries explicit,
- prefer compounding systems over isolated ones.
