# Idea Lifecycle

This document defines how ideas should move through the repository.

## Stage 1 — Spark
A brief capture of a potentially valuable idea.

Standard:
- quick to write,
- low commitment,
- clearly provisional.

Question:
- is this even worth future attention?

## Stage 2 — Incubating
The idea has enough promise to deserve a dedicated workspace.

Standard:
- has a named problem,
- has a tentative thesis,
- has at least one reason it may matter.

Question:
- is this promising enough to deserve deep discussion?

## Stage 3 — Dossier
The idea has been discussed seriously and now has a living design document.

A dossier is where we:
- refine the problem,
- test whether software is the right tool,
- identify failure modes,
- compare alternatives,
- record open questions,
- and decide whether the idea is strong or weak.

Question:
- does this deserve to become a specification?

## Stage 4 — Spec-ready
The idea is precise enough to begin formal specification work.

Standard:
- clear scope,
- clear leverage,
- clear boundaries,
- intelligible data model,
- explicit risks,
- plausible MVP.

Question:
- can this now be written as an implementation-grade spec?

## Stage 5 — Handoff-ready
The idea has a full spec and can be handed to Claude Code.

Standard:
- implementation brief is crisp,
- correctness constraints are explicit,
- dependencies are known,
- success criteria are testable,
- ambiguity is low enough for build work.

Question:
- should this now be built?

## Stage 6 — Parked / Rejected
The idea is intentionally not active.

Reasons may include:
- weak leverage,
- unclear bottleneck,
- too much scholarly risk,
- depends on missing substrate,
- strategically inferior to another idea,
- or simply not now.

## Operating Rules

1. Most ideas should spend meaningful time in dossier form.
2. Promotion is earned, not assumed.
3. Rejection is healthy repository hygiene.
4. A parked idea is not a failure; it is preserved judgment.
5. Do not bypass stages unless there is a very strong reason.
