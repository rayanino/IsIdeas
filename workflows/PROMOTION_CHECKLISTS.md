# Promotion Checklists

These checklists are mandatory gates, not advisory prose.

If an idea changes stage or enters the frontier, the change should be traceable to:

- a decision record,
- the relevant checklist criteria,
- and at least one critique artifact when independent challenge is required.

## Spark → Incubating

- is there a real bottleneck here?
- is the idea at least plausibly high-leverage?
- is it worth giving a dedicated workspace?

## Incubating → Dossier

- is the idea strong enough to deserve repeated serious discussion?
- is there enough substance to justify a living working document?
- is there at least one serious reason this could matter over years?

## Dossier → Spec-ready

- is the problem now precise?
- is the leverage case still strong after challenge?
- are the main boundaries visible?
- is the data model becoming intelligible?
- are the major failure modes named?
- is there a plausible MVP?
- is any blocking `kr` dependency explicit rather than assumed away?

## Spec-ready → Handoff-ready

- is the spec implementation-grade?
- are correctness constraints explicit?
- is the MVP scope crisp?
- are dependencies known?
- are success criteria testable?
- would implementation still need to guess about core behavior?

## Frontier Entry

- is the idea already at dossier stage or beyond?
- is there an independent critique artifact attached?
- is the idea under deep attention rather than merely visible?
- is the focus limit still respected?

## Park / Reject Check

- is the leverage weaker than expected?
- is the bottleneck unclear?
- is the software fit poor?
- is the scholarly-risk too high?
- does it depend on substrate that does not yet exist?
- is another idea strategically superior?
