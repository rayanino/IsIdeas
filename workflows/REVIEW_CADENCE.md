# Review Cadence

This repository needs deliberate review rhythms.
Without them, state labels drift, dossiers decay, and priority quietly becomes emotional rather than strategic.

## 1. Session-Level Review
After any serious session, review:
- what became clearer,
- what became weaker,
- what artifact must change,
- and whether the idea's stage should stay the same.

This is the minimum review loop.

## 2. Short-Horizon Review
At a regular short interval, review the currently active focus and the other actively attended ideas.

Recommended scope:
- `catalog/ACTIVE_FOCUS.md`
- `catalog/IDEA_REGISTRY.md`
- the dossier of the current primary focus

Questions:
- is the current focus still the right one?
- are active ideas still truly active?
- is any dossier drifting without a clear next move?

## 3. Portfolio Review
At a wider interval, review the portfolio as a whole.

Recommended scope:
- all serious ideas in the registry
- parked and rejected items worth re-checking
- open questions affecting multiple ideas
- the current priority board

Questions:
- which ideas have strengthened?
- which have weakened?
- which are blocked by missing substrate?
- which are strategically strong but not timely?
- is focus allocation still correct?

## 4. Architecture Review
At a slower interval, review the workshop itself.

Questions:
- are stage semantics still working?
- are artifacts remaining useful rather than ceremonial?
- is the registry still readable?
- are naming and focus rules being followed?
- does the repo still resist chaos as intended?

## 5. Triggered Review
Do not wait for cadence if a trigger appears.

Strong triggers include:
- a dossier feeling stale,
- repeated uncertainty about an idea's stage,
- too many active fronts,
- confusion about `kr` boundaries,
- or a strong new idea threatening to displace current focus.

## Rule
Cadence should support judgment, not become bureaucracy.
The point is not ritual.
The point is to keep the portfolio honest and strategically sharp.
