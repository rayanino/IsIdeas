# Staleness and Dormancy Rules

A repository can look active while actually decaying.
These rules help identify when an idea or artifact has gone stale.

## Stale Dossier Signals
A dossier is becoming stale if:
- it has no current judgment,
- it has no clear next move,
- its open questions are no longer meaningful,
- its state in the registry no longer matches its actual condition,
- or repeated sessions produce little sharpening.

## Dormant Idea Signals
An idea may be dormant rather than active if:
- it remains in the registry but receives no real attention,
- it is repeatedly deferred without explicit parking,
- or it consumes conceptual space without live strategic value.

## Required Response
When staleness is noticed, do one of the following explicitly:
- refresh the dossier,
- reduce its priority,
- park it,
- reject it,
- or move focus back onto it deliberately.

Do not let stale ideas linger in ambiguous semi-life.

## Registry Honesty Rule
The registry should reflect real state, not aspirational state.
If an idea is not truly active, its status and next move should say so.

## Artifact Hygiene Rule
An artifact that is no longer helping should be:
- updated,
- archived conceptually,
- or allowed to remain only if it still preserves useful judgment.

## Warning
Dormancy is not always bad.
Unacknowledged dormancy is bad.
